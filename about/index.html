The misunderstood problem

Most children are being taught to compute. Almost none are being taught to think mathematically.

This is the distinction that changes everything — and the one that almost every school, parent, and maths app gets wrong.

For two decades, Australian maths teaching has prioritised computation. The mechanical arithmetic. Times tables. Vertical addition. The drillable, testable, checkable skills.

Computation matters. It's the foundation that everything else sits on. But it isn't maths.

Maths is reasoning. It's recognising a pattern. It's choosing the right operation when the problem doesn't tell you which one. It's working out that if a shirt was reduced by 30% to $42, the original price was $60 — and being able to explain the logic.

A child can be a perfect calculator and a poor mathematical thinker. They get every arithmetic question right and freeze when a problem becomes multi-step or worded. This describes hundreds of thousands of Australian children — children whose parents believe they're doing well because the worksheets come back ticked.

Schools assess reasoning. Schools rarely have time to teach it individually. No app has built a serious diagnostic tool for it. Until Burrow.

Bloom's 2 Sigma Problem — and why AI finally solves it

In 1984, educational psychologist Benjamin Bloom published one of the most important findings in education history: the average student with a personal tutor performed two standard deviations better than classroom-taught peers.

That means the average tutored student outperformed 98% of classroom students. Bloom called it "an educational contribution of the greatest magnitude" — if we could scale it.

The problem was always cost. One-to-one tutoring costs $60–$100 per hour. Out of reach for most families. So for forty years, the finding sat as an open challenge.

A 2024 randomised controlled study found that AI tutoring produced learning gains more than twice as large as traditional classroom instruction — in less time. The 2 sigma problem, after four decades, is finally being solved at scale. Burrow is built on exactly this science.
